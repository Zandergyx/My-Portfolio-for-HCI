The Most Impactful Moments in my CS Educational Journey So Far

Roboto

I joined Roboto Coding July 2022. I will never forget my mentor, Mr Wong Yong Bin. He is the one who ignited my love for coding. Their program has a broad-based curriculum. I was introduced to the world of mBot, CoSpaces, Minecraft Education, Micro:Bit, Thunkable, HTML, CSS 3, Bootstrap, Python, Game Maker Studio, C++ and Unity. Though this course may seem to offer breadth, it lacks depth. However, it serves as a good start for me to build my knowledge onward and upward.

My Primary Reference Sites

GeeksForGeeks, The languages Documentations, StackOverflow; Any burning questions would involve utilizing ChatGPT Mini.

MyCodeSchool

I discovered a brilliant YouTube series on data structures and algorithms by MyCodeSchool. This series was founded in 2012 by Animesh Nayan and Harsha Suryanarayana, both legendary competitive programmers. However, because of a tragic accident, their production on MyCodeSchool lasted only 4+ years. This series provided explanations to fundamental concepts in easy-to-understand language. Simply brilliant.

CS50

This course was recommended to me by a parent from Mensa Junior as I was seeking a higher-level structured course so I may learn and fill in knowledge gaps. Professor Malan is an engaging lecturer. During his first lecture, he used blinking light bulbs to represent how the CPU in the computer uses bits to store information. The concept of binary was easy to understand as it was explained in a logical fashion. I was hooked and fascinated.

And There Are Times When Things Go Wrong….

Nowadays, I always use a Virtual Machine whenever I am running risky codes or when I am dealing with API calls. My previous laptop, an Acer, slowed down drastically because of my code, an API call. When I had to upgrade my laptop, I installed a virtual machine. 2 months ago, my uncle ran into a programming issue and wanted to discuss a way to resolve it with me. He tried to program an API Stock Market Project and made a mistake by calling ChatGPT in a While True Loop. I had to explain to him that a While True Loop will go on indefinitely as true stands for ‘Yes’ in English allowing the loop to go forever. Because of this, the laptop ran slower and slower. He also opened too many tabs of ChatGPT and almost deleted System 32. At the end, I had to help him restart his system. Although the laptop returned to normal function, my uncle lost his files.

So What’s Next…

I would hope to follow in the footsteps of Animesh and Harsha and become a competitive programmer. Making mistakes and learning from them, though heartbreaking at times, serves as a great experience. I crave for knowledge and am happy to work hard for it all.
